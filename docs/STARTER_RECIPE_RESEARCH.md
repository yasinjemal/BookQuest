# Source-Grounded Starter Recipe Research

**Status:** Phase 2 research only; recipe persistence/editor not enabled  
**Last updated:** 12 July 2026

These five recipe definitions are grounded in documents already uploaded to the
workspace. They validate that one versioned recipe model can support concise
instruction, practical projects, technical remediation and reflective coaching.
Generated material remains a draft requiring human approval.

Common contract fields: stable recipe ID/version, audience, objectives, duration,
lesson size, teaching style, tone/language/reading level, block mix, assessment,
pass/completion rule, source-trace policy, delivery/offline profile,
accommodations, credential behavior and safety boundaries.

## 1. Money Foundations Micro-course (`money-foundations/v1`)

**Source:** `2-sample-book.md` - money, banking, saving and interest.  
**Audience:** ages 14+, first-time financial-literacy learners.  
**Outcome:** explain money's three functions, bank spread/reserves, compound
interest and APR; compare two borrowing offers.  
**Shape:** 3 lessons, 5-7 minutes each; explanation -> worked example -> MCQ or
true/false -> recap. One compound-interest and one APR scenario.  
**Completion:** every lesson attempted and 70% server-graded score; unlimited
practice, no high-stakes credential.  
**Delivery:** text-first, fully offline, no AI needed after publication; plain
language and calculator-free alternatives.  
**Boundary:** educational examples, not personal financial advice.

## 2. AI Empire Project Sprint (`ai-empire-sprint/v1`)

**Source:** `1-The_9_AI_Powers_Final_Reordered.pdf` - mindset, prompting,
automation, writing, design and ethical use.  
**Audience:** aspiring creators/solopreneurs with basic smartphone/web skills.  
**Outcome:** write/iterate a role-task-context-constraint prompt, map one
automation, produce/edit one human-owned content artifact, and apply privacy,
fact-checking, copyright and transparency checks.  
**Shape:** selected modules rather than page-for-page conversion; story hook ->
source-backed explanation -> prompt lab -> practical artifact -> peer/self-review
checklist -> reflection. 60-90 minutes total in resumable 10-minute units.  
**Completion:** submit prompt iterations, automation map and edited artifact;
attest to ethical checklist. Practical work requires human/manager review rather
than automatic correctness.  
**Delivery:** low-bandwidth instructions and downloadable worksheets; external AI
tools optional with a manual/no-AI planning path.  
**Boundary:** never promise income, copy provider claims uncritically or send
sensitive/proprietary data to public models.

## 3. AI Safety Onboarding (`responsible-ai-onboarding/v1`)

**Source:** ethical compass and human-AI partnership sections of
`1-The_9_AI_Powers_Final_Reordered.pdf`.  
**Audience:** staff permitted to use generative AI at work.  
**Outcome:** recognize hallucination, privacy, copyright, bias and disclosure
risks; choose the safe action in workplace scenarios; explain why human authority
remains responsible.  
**Shape:** 20-minute compliance-style course: policy statement -> short scenario ->
decision -> source-linked explanation, ending in attestation.  
**Completion:** 80% across scenario questions plus versioned attestation; failed
items enter review. Expiry/renewal is organization policy, not baked into content.  
**Delivery:** offline-compatible except attestation synchronization; screen-reader
labels and no color-only risk signals.  
**Boundary:** template is not a legal-compliance claim; organization reviewer must
replace examples with approved policy and jurisdictional guidance.

## 4. Architecture Review to Remediation Plan (`architecture-remediation/v1`)

**Source:** `4-TRADEFEED_ARCHITECTURE_REVIEW.md_-_Google_Docs.pdf` - prioritized
security, performance, database, mobile, SEO and technical-debt findings.  
**Audience:** software engineers, technical leads and product managers.  
**Outcome:** distinguish severity from priority, locate trust boundaries, convert
a finding into threat/control/proof, and sequence remediation by risk/dependency.  
**Shape:** audit orientation -> five finding stations -> evidence-trace exercise ->
prioritization simulation -> remediation-plan practical task. Findings retain
their source section/page and uncertainty.  
**Completion:** 75% knowledge checks plus reviewer-approved plan containing owner,
deadline, acceptance proof and rollback.  
**Delivery:** text/table first; downloadable finding cards; code excerpts require
horizontal-scroll and accessible text alternatives.  
**Boundary:** do not expose live secrets or reproduce sensitive logs; review is a
dated snapshot, not certification that the system is secure.

## 5. First-Generation Wealth Reflection Path (`sovereign-wealth-path/v1`)

**Source:** `5-From_Scarcity_to_Sovereign__First-Gen_Wealth_for_the_Underdog.md` -
scarcity beliefs, wealth roots, attention, unstable-income budgeting, skills,
community, mobile finance, protection and legacy.  
**Audience:** adult first-generation wealth builders, especially irregular-income
and mobile-first learners.  
**Outcome:** identify one inherited money belief, build a variable-income ledger,
choose one skill/community action, apply digital-finance safety and draft a
90-day plan.  
**Shape:** 12 optional chapters grouped into Mindset, Stability, Growth and Legacy;
parable -> concept -> private journal -> practical worksheet -> reflection. No
public leaderboard or forced sharing of financial details.  
**Completion:** learner-controlled milestones and private portfolio; knowledge
checks only for objective safety/finance concepts, never for personal beliefs.  
**Delivery:** downloadable text/worksheets, local currency examples, long-translation
layouts, private journaling and skip-without-penalty for sensitive prompts.  
**Boundary:** no investment guarantees, shame, employability inference or request
for bank credentials; local financial/legal advice remains external.

## Research acceptance

Before shipping, each recipe must be instantiated against its named source,
reviewed for unsupported claims and source coverage, tested on mobile/offline and
with a screen reader, and versioned independently from generated course content.
